Probability & Naïve Bayes – Topic Explanation

1. Fundamentals of Probability

Random Experiment: An experiment whose outcome cannot be predicted with certainty.

Sample Space (S): The set of all possible outcomes of a random experiment.

Event: Any subset of the sample space.

Probability of an Event: Measures the likelihood of occurrence of an event.

P(A) = Favourable outcomes / Total outcomes

2. Laws of Probability

Addition Law of Probability

Used when finding the probability of occurrence of at least one event.

For overlapping events:

P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A ∩ B)

For mutually exclusive events:

P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B)

Multiplication Law of Probability

Used to find the probability that two or more events occur together.

P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B | A)

For independent events:

P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B)

3. Conditional Probability

The probability of an event occurring given that another event has already occurred.

P(A | B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B)

Used in:

  • Communication systems
  • Spam filtering
  • Medical diagnosis

4. Mutually Exclusive Events

Events that cannot occur simultaneously.

A ∩ B = ∅

Example: Getting a head and a tail in a single coin toss.

5. Independent Events

Events where occurrence of one does not affect the other.

P(A | B) = P(A)

Example: Coin toss and die roll.

6. Tree Diagrams & Sample Space Construction

Tree diagrams are graphical tools used to:

  • Represent sequential events
  • Generate sample spaces
  • Visualize conditional probabilities

Applications:

  • Switch configurations
  • Communication channels
  • Multi-stage experiments

7. Complementary Events

The complement of event A is the event that A does not occur.

P(Aᶜ) = 1 − P(A)

Used in:

  • “At least once” problems
  • Reliability analysis
  • Detection and failure systems

8. Impossible and Certain Events

Impossible event: Probability = 0

Certain event: Probability = 1

Used to identify logically invalid outcomes.

9. Bayes’ Theorem

Used to reverse conditional probability.

P(A | B) = [P(B | A) × P(A)] / P(B)

Essential in:

  • Spam filters
  • AI systems
  • Medical testing
  • Fault diagnosis

10. Total Probability Theorem

Used when an event depends on multiple mutually exclusive causes.

P(B) = Σ P(B | Aᵢ) × P(Aᵢ)

Forms the base for Bayes’ theorem.

11. Probability of Error

Used in:

  • Communication systems
  • Signal transmission
  • AI and robotics

Error occurs when:

Received signal ≠ Transmitted signal

12. Naïve Bayesian Classification

A supervised machine learning algorithm based on Bayes’ theorem.

Key Assumption: Attributes are conditionally independent.

Classification Rule:

P(Class | X) ∝ P(Class) × ∏ P(Attribute | Class)

Used in:

  • Spam detection
  • Pattern recognition
  • Medical diagnosis
  • Data mining

13. Real-World Applications of Probability

  • Artificial intelligence decision accuracy
  • Robot obstacle detection
  • Virus spread modeling
  • Communication channel noise
  • Spam filtering
  • Data classification
